# Movement Diagnosis Protocol

The three movements — Awareness, Familiarity, Value — describe where an account sits in its relationship with you. Each movement has its own character, its own appropriate actions, and its own metrics. The machine diagnoses the movement before recommending any action.

## The Fundamental Rule

**You can compress movements but you cannot skip them.**

A prospect who has not experienced Awareness will ignore Familiarity-stage outreach. A prospect who has not experienced Familiarity will resist Value-stage engagement regardless of product fit. Technical excellence without trust is insufficient. The buyer's emotional progression — curiosity → trust → confidence — must be respected.

## Diagnostic Questions

### Awareness Assessment
Ask these about the account. Answer from available data.

1. Would they recognize the company name? (Check: content consumption, website visits, ad impressions, brand search queries)
2. Have they consumed any content? (Check: content engagement data, email opens, webinar attendance)
3. Would they accept a connection request from a seller? (Check: LinkedIn engagement, social signals)
4. Have they interacted with the brand anywhere? (Check: event attendance, community participation, support tickets)

**If mostly NO → Account is in pre-Awareness.** Actions: content distribution, thought leadership, SEO/AI discoverability, industry event presence. Do not outreach directly — they will ignore you.

**If mostly YES → Account has Awareness.** Proceed to Familiarity assessment.

### Familiarity Assessment
1. Would they take a call from a specific seller? (Check: prior engagement, response to outreach)
2. Has a seller provided value without asking for anything? (Check: interaction history — value provocations sent, insights shared)
3. Do they engage with seller content personally? (Check: likes, comments, shares, replies)
4. Would they describe the seller as helpful rather than "salesperson"? (Check: tone of interactions, referral language)

**If mostly NO → Account is in Awareness, not Familiarity.** Actions: value provocations, personalized insights, warm introductions, relationship building. No demo requests. No meeting asks. No sales pressure. Pure value delivery.

**If mostly YES → Account has Familiarity.** Proceed to Value readiness.

### Value Readiness Assessment
1. Is there an active need the solution can address? (Check: signals — earnings calls, job postings, news, competitive moves)
2. Is there budget or willingness to create budget? (Check: fiscal year timing, budget signals, executive sponsorship)
3. Are decision-makers accessible? (Check: engagement levels, organizational mapping)
4. Would they invest time in a proof-of-value? (Check: expressed interest, competitive dynamics, urgency signals)

**If mostly YES → Account is ready for Value movement.** Actions: custom demonstrations, proof of value, pilots, quick wins. Demonstrate, don't describe.

**If mostly NO → Account needs more Familiarity.** Return to Familiarity actions. Do not force Value-stage engagement.

## Action Protocols by Movement

### Awareness Actions
**Goal:** Be discoverable when the prospect begins exploring a problem you can solve.

- Publish content that demonstrates domain expertise (not product marketing)
- Ensure discoverability through AI assistants, search engines, industry forums
- Maintain industry presence through events, publications, analyst relationships
- Monitor for signals that an account is entering exploration mode

**Metrics:** Content reach, brand search volume, share of voice, inbound traffic from target accounts.

**Time investment per account:** Minimal (1-2 hours/month through scalable content). Awareness activities operate at portfolio level, not account level.

### Familiarity Actions
**Goal:** Build trust through consistent value delivery without asking for anything.

12-Month Familiarity Plan template:
- **Month 1:** Personalized insight based on their recent earnings or announcement
- **Month 2:** Relevant industry research (not vendor propaganda)
- **Month 3:** Introduction to a peer who faced a similar challenge
- **Month 4:** Brief analysis of a trend affecting their industry
- **Month 5:** Invitation to an exclusive event or community
- **Month 6:** Custom benchmark data for their segment
- **Month 7:** Article or thought piece relevant to their stated priorities
- **Month 8:** Introduction to an analyst or expert in their space
- **Month 9:** Brief on a regulatory or competitive development affecting them
- **Month 10:** Updated analysis reflecting changes since Month 1
- **Month 11:** Invitation to contribute to or review upcoming research
- **Month 12:** Year-in-review analysis of their industry with forward outlook

**Total investment per account:** ~12 hours over 12 months.

**Critical absence:** No demo requests. No meeting asks. No sales pressure. If you're asking for something, you're not building Familiarity — you're selling. Familiarity is the absence of selling in the presence of value.

**Metrics:** Response rate to outreach, engagement depth, relationship temperature change, time-to-opportunity when Value signals emerge.

**Compression for fast cycles (90 days):**
- Weeks 1-2: Rapid research synthesis + 2-3 best content pieces + peer reference
- Weeks 3-6: Insight package + quick win without commitment + executive introduction
- Weeks 7-12: Full Value movement

You can accelerate Familiarity but you cannot eliminate it.

### Value Actions
**Goal:** Demonstrate outcomes before the contract is signed.

- Build proof-of-value using the prospect's actual data or close proxies
- Create custom demonstrations that show their specific situation improved
- Run pilots or quick wins that produce measurable results
- Deliver business cases with the prospect's own numbers

**The core difference:** Traditional selling convinces the buyer that value will result from purchase. Forward-deployed selling demonstrates value before purchase. The conversation shifts from "this is what we could do" to "this is what we have done."

**Metrics:** Proof-of-value completion rate, POV-to-proposal conversion, deal size from POV-originated opportunities, cycle time from POV to close.

## The Buyer's Emotional Journey

Each movement produces a specific emotional state in the buyer. The machine monitors for these states and adjusts its approach accordingly.

**During Awareness:** "I keep seeing this company's content. They seem to understand the problems I'm facing. I should remember them."
→ The buyer feels: curiosity, recognition, mental filing.
→ Machine action: Continue creating relevant, discoverable content. Do not escalate.

**During Familiarity:** "This person keeps sending me useful things without asking for anything. They seem genuinely interested in my success. I trust them."
→ The buyer feels: gratitude, trust, relationship warmth.
→ Machine action: Continue value delivery. Monitor for Value readiness signals. Do not ask for meetings.

**During Value:** "They've already shown me what's possible. This isn't a pitch — it's a preview of working together. I've seen the value with my own eyes."
→ The buyer feels: confidence, reduced risk, forward momentum.
→ Machine action: Demonstrate. Make saying yes obvious. Remove remaining friction.

**Key insight:** By the time the account reaches Value, the emotional work should already be complete. If the machine is still building trust in the Value movement, Familiarity was insufficient. Diagnose backward and adjust.

## Movement Transition Signals

The machine watches for signals that an account is ready to advance:

**Awareness → Familiarity:**
- Prospect engages with content multiple times
- Prospect connects with seller on LinkedIn
- Prospect attends a company event
- Prospect mentions company in a peer conversation

**Familiarity → Value:**
- Prospect asks a specific question about capabilities
- Prospect shares an internal challenge unprompted
- Prospect introduces other stakeholders
- Prospect references competitive evaluation
- Budget signal fires (fiscal year, new initiative, board mandate)

**Regression signals** (account moving backward):
- Champion goes dark after engagement
- Decision-maker change resets relationships
- Budget freeze or strategic pivot
- Competitive loss at a reference account

When regression signals fire, the machine re-diagnoses and adjusts. Do not continue Value actions when the account has regressed to Familiarity or Awareness.

## The Learning Loop

Each movement generates information that improves the others:

- **Awareness activities** reveal what topics and problems resonate → informs Familiarity content
- **Familiarity activities** reveal what individual prospects care about → informs Value demonstration focus
- **Value activities** reveal what actually works and delivers → informs Awareness content

The machine captures these learnings and feeds them back across all accounts, not just the originating account. Intelligence that isn't captured is intelligence lost.
